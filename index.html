Founder note

Built for the part of cooking most apps skip.

I built Pantry & Plate™ because meal planning, pantry tracking, and grocery lists were always split across different tools. This brings them into one household workflow: save the recipes you actually cook, plan from what is already home, and shop only for what is missing.

Trust

Your recipes stay yours.

Private household recipes are intended to be visible only to you and your household members. Pantry & Plate™ does not sell, publish, or use private household recipes for marketing or advertising.

AI recipe extraction and generation run only when you choose to use those features. Camera and photo access are used only for user-started actions such as barcode scanning, recipe capture, or selected uploads.

FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Where can I download Pantry & Plate?

Pantry & Plate™ is now available on the App Store. Google Play link coming soon.

Is Google Play available?

A public Google Play link is not available yet. We will add it to this site when it is ready, and you can email support@pantryandplate.app with availability questions.

Can I send product feedback?

Yes. Email support@pantryandplate.app with feedback, questions, or issues. Feedback helps improve the pantry, recipe, meal-planning, and shopping-list workflow.

Does Pantry & Plate use AI?

Pantry & Plate may use AI for user-initiated recipe extraction or generation. That means prompts, links, or images are processed only when you choose to use those features.

Are private recipes shared publicly?

No. Private household recipes are intended for you and your household members. Any public sharing should be explicit, opt-in, and clearly labeled.

Can Pantry & Plate help reduce duplicate grocery purchases?

That is one of the main goals. The app is designed to compare planned meals against pantry context so shopping lists focus on missing items.
