Tutorial

Your first successful week in Perfect Pantry

This guide is optimized for new users: set up once, plan quickly, then maintain with light daily updates.

Quick start Pantry setup Build cookbook Plan the week Generate shopping list Maintenance habits Troubleshooting

0 to useful in 10 minutes

Quick start checklist

  1. Create your account and complete onboarding prompts.
  2. Add 10 to 20 pantry staples you buy most often.
  3. Save 5 recipes your household will actually eat this week.
  4. Add those recipes to the Plan tab by day.
  5. Open Shop and review what still needs to be purchased.

What "good" looks like after day one

  • Your pantry has enough data to prevent obvious duplicates.
  • Your weekly plan has at least 3 committed dinners.
  • Your shopping list is focused and not overloaded with staples.

Step 1

Set pantry truth first

Pantry quality determines plan and shopping quality. Start here before trying to automate your week.

Recommended setup order

  1. Add shelf-stable staples first (grains, oils, canned goods).
  2. Add high-turn items (eggs, milk, produce) second.
  3. Use UPC scanner for packaged items when available.
  4. Add optional notes for preferred brands and quantities.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Skipping quantities for items you frequently overbuy.
  • Ignoring expiration data for short-life ingredients.
  • Leaving duplicate pantry entries unmerged after a big restock.

Step 2

Build a realistic cookbook

Avoid collecting inspiration-only recipes. Save recipes your household can repeat with your normal grocery rhythm.

Save by effort

Tag weeknight recipes separately from weekend projects.

Balance reuse

Favor ingredient overlap to reduce waste and simplify lists.

Cook mode ready

Prioritize recipes that are easy to execute in the cooking view.

Step 3

Plan before you shop

The Plan tab is where you make intentional tradeoffs: time, leftovers, pantry coverage, and household preferences.

1

Anchor fixed nights

Block known constraints first (late work, activities, events).

2

Drop in low-friction meals

Use fast repeats for busy days to keep the plan stable.

3

Check pantry coverage

Confirm ingredient availability before finalizing the week.

4

Adjust once, then commit

Avoid daily replanning by locking a realistic baseline.

Step 4

Shop the gap, not the whole pantry

Pre-trip process

  1. Review generated items from your active meal plan.
  2. Add household extras that are not meal-driven.
  3. Remove anything already covered by pantry stock.
  4. Sort for your normal store route to reduce trip time.

During trip

  • Check off items in real time to keep sync clean.
  • Use barcode scan where helpful for exact product matching.
  • Update pantry after restock to keep next plan accurate.

Weekly maintenance

15-minute routine that keeps the app valuable

  • Sunday: set the week plan and generate shopping list.
  • Midweek: quick pantry cleanup and remove consumed items.
  • After grocery trip: confirm restocks and clear completed list items.

Troubleshooting

If something feels off

My list still has duplicates.

Verify pantry quantities first, then regenerate the list from your final meal plan state.

Pantry coverage does not look right.

Confirm recipe ingredients are fully saved and that pantry item names are normalized consistently.

I want to request a workflow feature.

Send it through Support with your current routine and where friction happens.
